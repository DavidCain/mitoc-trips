Individual Safety Gear (All Participants)

Essentials

  • Clothing
  • Footwear
  • Food & water
  • Whistle
  • Compass
  • Map (photocopy provided by leader is sufficient)
  • Headlamp or flashlight

Above-treeline Gear

  • Goggles
  • Balaclava. When worn together, goggles + balaclava should leave no exposed skin

Traction/Flotation

Depending on your trip and conditions, you will bring different forms of traction and flotation. For example:

  • Microspikes (most trips)
  • Snowshoes (optional - recent snow or unpacked trails)
  • Crampons (optional - icy or steep terrain)

Special Gear

Depending on your trip, there may be some specialized gear that should be brought along. For example:

  • Skis
  • Fatbikes
  • Ice tools
  • Etc.

Group Safety Gear - Hiking

Trip leaders must ensure that collectively their group carries the following gear according to trip type.

A Trip

For A Trips the following gear must be carried by the group at all times

  • One MITOC first aid kit
  • Extra layers (clothing)
  • Extra food and water
  • Two foam pads
  • Duct tape or climbing tape
  • Parachute cord or webbing

B Trip

For B Trips the following gear must be carried by the group at all times

  • All the group gear required for an A Trip +
  • Two sleeping bags (one winter (15F) rated and one three-season (35F) rated minimum)
  • Emergency shelter for 2 people. For example, any of:
    • Bothy bag (at least 2p)
    • Tent (at least 2p)
    • Bivy x2 (such as the Outdoor Research Alpine Bivy, not an emergency blanket)
  • A winter-rated stove with pot, lid, fuel, and fire starters (optional)

C Trips

For C Trips the following gear must be carried by the group at all times

  • All the group gear required for a B Trip +
  • Two winter-rated (rated 15F or better) sleeping bags (instead of the one winter and one three-season bag for a B Trip)
  • One emergency shelter for 2 people (like B trip, but any tents must be a 4-season/mountaineering tent)
  • One additional bothy bag

Group Safety Gear - Overnights

For overnight trips involving camping in the backcountry, trip leaders must ensure that collectively their group carries the following gear

  • All the group gear required for a C Trip +
  • Two shovels
  • Adequate shelter for everyone - don't overstuff tents

Group Safety Gear - Ice Climbing

On Ice Climbing Trips, leaders must ensure that collectively their group has the following gear available per trip rating

AI Trip

For AI Trips the following items must be carried by the group

  • One MITOC first aid kit
  • Extra layers (clothing)
  • Extra food and water
  • One foam pad
  • Duct tape or climbing tape
  • Parachute cord or webbing

while the following items may be left in the car at the trailhead

  • One winter-rated sleeping bag
  • One shelter
  • A winter-rated stove with pot, lid, fuel, and fire starters (optional)

BI Trip

For BI Trips, AI Trip group gear is required to be carried by the group at all times (none of it may be left in the car).

CI Trip

For CI Trips trip leaders must provide the WSC with a proposed safety gear list for approval that is specific to the proposed trip.

Group Safety Gear - Skiing

On Backcountry Skiing Trips, leaders must ensure that collectively their group has the following gear available per trip rating

AS Trip

For AS Trips the following items must be carried by the group

  • One MITOC first aid kit
  • One MITOC ski repair kit
  • Extra layers (clothing)
  • Extra food and water
  • One foam pads
  • Duct tape or climbing tape
  • Parachute cord or webbing

while the following items may be left in the car at the trailhead

  • One winter-rated sleeping bag
  • One shelter
  • A winter-rated stove with pot, lid, fuel, and fire starters (optional)

BS Trip

For BS Trips, AS Trip group gear is required to be carried by the group at all times (none of it may be left in the car).

CS Trip

For CS Trips trip leaders must provide the WSC with a proposed safety gear list for approval that is specific to the proposed trip.
