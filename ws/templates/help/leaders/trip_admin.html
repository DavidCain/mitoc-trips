What is the trip admin view?

The trip administration view is similar to the trip-viewing page that everyone else sees, but it includes some features available only to the trip's creator and the leaders on the trip. Key features include the ability to:

  • Edit trip details
  • Modify the participant list
  • View trip medical information
  • Submit trip itinerary to the WIMP
  • Give participant feedback

To explore the trip admin interface, you can navigate to your trip's viewing page (e.g. through upcoming trips). You'll be automatically redirected to the trip administration page. You can also go directly to trip administration by viewing trips you're leading.

What do I need to do before leaving on my trip?

Disclaimer: These are the steps that need to take place on the web site. This does not include standard pre-trip procedures.

  • Ensure that the participant list is accurate: remove anyone who's not on your trip, and manually add anyone who doesn't appear on the participant roster.
  • Make sure that all participant (and leader) information is accurate.
  • Submit your trip itinerary to the WIMP.

How do I view everyone's contact or medical information?

You can always click on a participant's name for a detailed summary of their information. However, if you'd like to see a summary of everyone on your trip (leaders included), click on "show WIMP info." The linked page will include medical information and contact information for each person on your trip.

What do I put in the trip itinerary?

Your trip itinerary should contain everything that a rescue party would want to know. It should contain at minimum which cars are driving for your trip, a complete list of participants, and accurate medical information for everyone. It should also contain a detailed description of your group's intentions. Your plans may change, so you are encouraged to update your itinerary as often as needed to ensure it's up-to-date. The WIMP will always have access to the most recent trip itinerary.

How do signups work?

A "signup" denotes that a participant has shown interest in your trip. When your trip is in lottery mode, participants can freely sign up without being placed on your trip or its wait list. The lottery algorithm will later decide if they should be placed on your trip (for more information, see the lottery help pages).

When your trip is in "first-come, first-serve" mode, any participants signing up will immediately be placed on your trip or on the next available spot on the waiting list.

How does the waitlist work?

The waitlist is an ordered queue of participant signups. Whenever a spot opens up on your trip, the first signup from the queue is automatically added to your trip. When a participant is placed on a trip, he or she receives an email urging them to contact that trip's leaders.

How is the waitlist ordered?

Normally, when a signup is added to the waitlist, that signup goes to the bottom. However, under certain circumstances, the signup can be added towards the top. For example, if a participant loses their trip spot to a driver, they will go to the top of the list. Additionally, leaders can add participants directly to the trip. If the trip is full, leader-added participants will be placed towards to the top of the waiting list.

How do leader-added signups work?

Normally, participants will sign themselves up for a trip. However, under certain circumstances, a leader may want to sign up participants. For example, a participant may be a last-minute addition, and the leader wants to ensure that the participant list is accurate for the WIMP form. Alternatively, a given participant may be needed so that the trip has enough drivers. The leader can add this participant (or bump an existing signup) so that he or she bypasses the waiting list queue.

When using the manual participant signup form, participants are normally placed above existing waitlist signups, but below other prioritized signups (such as participants who were bumped due to lack of drivers). Leaders can optionally override this behavior so that the signup in question goes to the absolute first position in the waitlist.

Can I create a trip where I'm not a leader?

Yes. You can create trips for other leaders and retain the ability to administer that trip.
