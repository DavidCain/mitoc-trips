How Leader Ratings are Assigned

Initial Terrain Ratings: (co)A, (co)B, (co)C

To receive a Winter School leader rating you should have some winter hiking experience, and some group management experience (could be leading summer hikes).

If you are a new Winter School leader you start with only a co-rating to learn how MITOC does things from an established full leader. If you have substantial winter hiking experience outside of MITOC we will ask you to have your co-leader the first weekend send a reference for upgrade to full leader status once you are familiar with the MITOC way of doing things.

For a coB rating you should have experience travelling in B terrain. Most trips are B trips so we try to assign coB ratings where possible, and most winter hiking experience falls within B territory anyway.

For a coC rating you should know how to self-arrest from all 4 positions, proper ice axe and crampon use, be able to navigate in poor visibility, and properly layer above treeline.

If you do not have current WFA (or higher) you cannot be a full B or C leader.

Initial Activity Ratings: (co)I, (co)S

coI leaders are likely experienced rock climbers, good at building TR anchors, and have experience following ice. Most often they are MITOC rock climbing leaders or involved in the rock climbing program. Full Ice leaders are required to be comfortable leading WI3 as we expect full ice leaders to know how to read ice conditions and have substantial ice climbing experience.

Rating Upgrades

Upgrades work on a reference system. If you are planning on requesting a recommendation for an upgrade, you should let your co-leaders know while planning your trip. You should try to handle as much of the organization of the trip as possible and be sure to lead the group at times while in the wilderness to demonstrate your ability!

To move from coB to B or coC to C you should be leading that level trip, emailing the WSC to let us know you want to upgrade, and having your co-leaders emailing the WSC references shortly after your trip is finished. We typically ask for two favorable references (from two different trips) for an upgrade. We are looking for good group management and decision making, a clear understanding of MITOC rules, and the ability to organize a successful trip!

If you are a coB rated leader who wants a coC rating you should participate as a participant on a C trip and ask your leaders for references. We are looking to see that you are familiar with the issues of above treeline travel, are able to take care of yourself, and have experience using traction.
